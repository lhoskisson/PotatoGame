Team Reflection​

​   Throughout building Veggie-Coup, our team worked very well together, assigned tasks based on each person’s strengths, and attended each meeting to reflect on our work as well as determine what steps each person will need to take for the next stages of the game. While working on this game throughout the quarter, one thing that our team did really well was communicating with each other. Our communication was the key to the success of our game. Our team did a really good job asking for help whenever they get stuck implementing a feature for the game. 
  During the team meetings, each team member participated in brainstorming ideas for additional features to implement in the game. If a member was unable to attend a meeting, they would inform the team in a timely manner. After the meeting, a team member would include a summary of the meeting in the development log, so that each team member was aware of what the meeting was about and each member’s responsibilities for the next tasks. The majority of our communication took place through discord, the team meetings either in person or through discord voice channels, through the development log, and Kanban board. This ensured that each team member was aware of the progress each team member was making on their task.

     If we had another 2-weeks to work on the game the 3 highest priority items would be:      
1.To create a long-term goal for players to keep them more engaged in the game. 
2. Implement more enemy types to add to the game difficulty. The new enemies could include a close-range pumpkin enemy.
 3. To add more map objects and obstacles to break up the player and enemy movements. This would also add to the strategic side of the game for the players. The new obstacles would include rocks, trees, fences, and buildings.
